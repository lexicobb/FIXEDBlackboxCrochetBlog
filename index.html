Crochet Pattern of the Week: Granny Square

This week's pattern is the classic granny square! Granny squares are a staple in the crochet world and are a great way to use up leftover yarn. They can be made into all sorts of things, from blankets and pillows to bags and scarves.

To make a granny square, you'll need:

  • Yarn in your choice of color(s)
  • A crochet hook in the appropriate size for your yarn
  • Scissors
  • A yarn needle

Here's a simple pattern to get you started:

Chain 4 and join with a slip stitch to form a ring.

Round 1: Chain 3 (counts as first double crochet), 2 double crochet in ring, *chain 2, 3 double crochet in ring; repeat from \* 2 more times, chain 2, join with a slip stitch in top of chain 3.

Round 2: Slip stitch in next 2 double crochet and in next chain-2 space, chain 3 (counts as first double crochet), 2 double crochet in same space, *chain 2, 3 double crochet in next chain-2 space; repeat from \* 2 more times, chain 2, join with a slip stitch in top of chain 3.

Repeat round 2 until your granny square is the desired size.

Crochet Tip of the Week: Blocking

Blocking is a technique used to shape and smooth out crochet pieces. It can make a big difference in the final appearance of your project. To block a piece, you'll need:

  • A blocking mat or a towel
  • Pins
  • A spray bottle filled with water

Here's how to block a piece:

  1. Lay your crochet piece out on the blocking mat or towel.
  2. Pin the piece in place, making sure to stretch it out to the desired shape and size.
  3. Spray the piece with water until it's damp.
  4. Let the piece dry completely.

Blocking can be especially helpful for lace patterns and for pieces that have curled or distorted during the crocheting process.
